    Early Life

    David L. Ramsey III was born in Antioch, Tennessee, in September 1960. He received his diploma from the College of Business Administration at the University of Tennessee in 1982.

    When he was 26 years old, he had amassed a portfolio worth over $4 million. Ramsey had to file for bankruptcy after receiving a 90-day deadline to pay a bank more than $1 million.

    Dave Ramsey started counseling couples in his church while studying consumer financial issues. Using what he had discovered, he created a formula, and in 1992, he released his book, Financial Peace.

    More so, he debuted on the radio in 1992 as a co-host of The Money Game. The Dave Ramsey Show is carried on more than 500 radio stations across the United States and Canada. The New York Times bestseller list includes four of Ramsey’s works.

    He is renowned for presenting a Christian viewpoint on money. He received the 2009 Network/Syndicated Personality of the Year Marconi Award. He established the Lampo Group, Inc., a company that presently employs over 350 people and is debt-free.

    Dave Ramsey established Financial Peace University, a course that promotes financial education and is frequently taught in churches. According to current reports, Dave is the sole owner of a real estate portfolio worth $150 million.

    Dave Ramsey’s Career

    Ramsey began his radio career in 1992 as a co-host of The Money Game with Primerica’s Roy Matlock. This Nashville radio station’s local program eventually evolved into The Dave Ramsey Show.

    The Dave Ramsey Show, Ramsey’s nationally syndicated radio show, is broadcast live on YouTube, on more than 500 radio stations across the United States and Canada, as a podcast on IHeartRadio, on the Dave Ramsey Show iOS app, and on DaveRamsey.com.

    He has been on a variety of media platforms, such as “The Oprah Winfrey Show,” “60 Minutes,” and “The Early Show.” He taped six unaired episodes of The Dave Ramsey Project for CBS in addition to the pilot.
