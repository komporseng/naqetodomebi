    Kendall Jenner thinks 'Kardashian Curse' concept is 'offensive' and that the blame is on the men

    29 Aug 2024, 00:00

    Kendall Jenner pushed back at the notion of a ‘Kardashian Curse’ during Sunday’s second part of the Keeping Up With the Kardashians: The Final Curtain reunion on E! Sunday.

    The 25-year-old model and reality star responded to host Andy Cohen after he brought up the notion that the females in the famed family have harmed the careers of the men they’re involved with, with NBA alum Lamar Odom and musical superstar Kanye West among the most frequent examples cited amid their personal ups-and-downs.

    ‘You guys have talked about this, it’s like the men have trouble after you,’ Cohen said.

    The latest: Kendall Jenner, 25, pushed back at the notion of a ‘Kardashian Curse’ during Sunday’s second part of the Keeping Up With the Kardashians: The Final Curtain reunion on E! Sunday

    Kendall said she didn’t understand how she and her sisters could be blamed for the respective issues of their exes.

    ‘What I don’t like about this narrative is that the blame is on us,’ the Los Angeles native said. ‘I think the men need to take that responsibility.’

    The eldest daughter of Caitlyn and Kris Jenner described her family as ‘extremely giving and caring and loving women,’ a trait that extends to their love lives.

    ‘And when there is a man in our lives we like, give everything to them, and as a family we all love them so much,’ Kendall said. ‘It’s almost offensive, personally.’

    Closer look: The supermodel appeared alongside sisters Kourtney and Kim Kardashian on the special

    Kendall’s relatives jumped in to support her claim, as Kim downplayed any notion that West has struggled, saying that the Grammy-winning artist is ‘doing pretty well,’ while Kylie noted that her longtime beau rapper Travis Scott ‘is doing great.’

    Mom Kris Jenner noted that amid their relationships, their partners achieved huge feats including ‘two NBA championships (amid Khloe Kardashian’s relationship with former Los Angeles Laker Odom) and a Super Bowl (referring to Scott’s performance in 2019 at the big game).

    ‘Of course, factually, it doesn’t add up,’ said Kendall, who is currently linked to Phoenix Suns superstar Devin Booker.

    On hand for the reunion was Kourtney Kardashian’s ex Scott Disick, who said that he doesn’t believe there’s any curse associated with the famed family.

    Kendall said of the idea of a curse, ‘Of course, factually, it doesn’t add up’

    Kendall said in the reunion gathering that she’s tended to keep her romantic entanglements private after seeing the scrutiny her older sisters went through in their romances.

    ‘Kylie and I have had the opportunity to watch our older sisters go through marriages and relationships and breakups and all these things, and do them pretty publicly,’ she said. ‘And no offense to you guys at all, but I think it was personal preference from a really young age that I didn’t want to.’

    She said that it ‘makes [her] life a lot easier and our relationship a lot better, to be completely honest,’ noting, ‘It’s a private matter, it’s not really for anybody else to judge or know.’ 

    Kendall is currently linked to Phoenix Suns superstar Devin Booker, and has past been linked to Philadelphia 76ers star Ben Simmons

    The sisters have been in a number of high-profile relationships, with some men who have had problems in the limelight in the wake of their splits.

    Disick, who shares three kids with Kourtney, has past had rehab stints in 2015 and 2020, respectively. Odom in October of 2015 fell into a coma following a drug overdose at a Nevada brothel, an incident which led Khloe to delay their then-ongoing divorce proceedings. 

    Kylie Jenner’s ex Tyga has past made headlines amid court battles linked to financial issues, while Kim’s ex-husband Kris Humphries had past bemoaned being booed by NBA fans over his short-lived marriage to the starlet.
