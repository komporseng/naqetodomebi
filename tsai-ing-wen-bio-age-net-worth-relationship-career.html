    How did Tsai Ing-wen start her Professional Career?

    Tsai Ing-wen first began out as an instructor at the law departments of Soochow University and National Chengchi University in Taipei. She commenced her governmental service in the early 1990s and, during the Kuomintang (KMT) regime, was instrumental in trade negotiations about Taiwan’s accession to the World Trade Organization (WTO).

    Tasi assumed the position of Minister of the Mainland Affairs Council after the Democratic Progressive Party’s (DPP) overthrow in 2000.

    Subsequently, in 2004, Ing-wen endorsed the Democratic Progressive Party (DPP) and was granted the party’s nomination to contest the legislative election of that year. Furthermore, she was selected for the role of vice president of the Executive Yuan in 2006.

    She was also the chairwoman of the Consumer Protection Commission at the time. Since then, she has escalated tensions with the Chinese mainland and violated convention by initiating communication with the United States. Once more, Tsai won the general election of 2020 with over 57% of the vote.

    Tsai is the inaugural president elected by the people of Taipei and the second president of the Democratic Progressive Party. Before this election, he had not served as mayor of the city.

    For managing the COVID-19 pandemic and resisting pressure from the PRC government, she has garnered international acclaim. She resigned as leader of the DPP in November 2022, citing the party’s abysmal performance in the month’s municipal elections.

    In recognition of her contributions, Ing-wen has been bestowed with a multitude of honors and awards. In 2016, she was bestowed with the Grand Collar of the National Order of Merit. She was bestowed with the Grand Cross with Gold Star of the Doctor Jose Matias Delgado National Order the subsequent year.

    2018 also saw the awarding of the Grand Cross of the National Order of Honor and Merit and the Order of Belize. She was bestowed with the Order of St. Christopher and St. Nevis in 2019. In 2020, Time magazine included her on its list of the most influential individuals.
